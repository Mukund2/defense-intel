| **Autonomous Systems / Drones** | $9.8B directed to autonomous/unmanned | Replicator Initiative ($500M) | HIGH - DIU/AFWERX pathways |
| **AI/ML for Defense** | $1.8B direct + embedded across services | $66B total DoD IT budget | HIGH - CDAO Tradewinds marketplace |

### Counter-UAS Market Deep Dive (Hottest Area for Small Companies)
- **Global market:** $6.64B in 2025, projected $20.31B by 2030 (CAGR 25.1%)
- **US DoD allocation FY2026:** $3.1B (43% single-year increase)
- **AI-enhanced kinetic defeat subsegment:** $600M in 2025, up to $4.1B by 2030
- **Key drivers:** Ukraine battlefield validation, China drone swarm investments, FIFA 2026 security ($625M FEMA grant program)
- **Small company leaders:** Chaos Industries ($4.5B valuation), DroneShield, Dedrone

### AI/ML for Defense Deep Dive
- **Global AI military market:** $9.31B in 2024, CAGR 13.0% through 2030
- **DoD AI spending:** $1.8B direct request + pervasive across $66B IT budget
- **CDAO awarded $200M in 2025** to commercial AI providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, xAI)
- **DIU FY2026 budget:** $979M
- **Top startup recipients:** C3.AI, Shield AI, Scale AI, Vannevar Labs, Saildrone, Skydio
- **Key application areas:** Targeting, threat detection, mission planning, simulation, logistics, battlefield decision-making ("AI for Maneuver and Fires" funded at $88M)

### Space/SpaceWERX Deep Dive
- **SpaceWERX annual budget:** $460M
- **2025 STRATFI selections:** 8 companies for $440M combined (gov + private funding)
- **2023 Open Topic Phase II:** 142 contracts, projected value $151M
- **Focus areas:** Satellite servicing, missile tracking, rapid launch, space domain awareness

### Cybersecurity / CMMC Opportunity
- **338,000 contractors and subcontractors** impacted by CMMC rule (effective Nov 10, 2025)
- **~230,000 small entities** affected
- **210,000 contractors** fall into Level 1 (annual self-assessment)
- **118,000 entities** must obtain third-party certification
- **Assessor shortage** creates opportunities for CMMC assessment organizations
- **Compliance tool market** is massive and growing

### Defense Tech VC Investment (Context for What's Hot)
- **2024:** $3B in 102 deals to VC-backed defense startups (11% uptick from 2023)
- **2025:** Record-breaking $49.1B raised by defense tech startups (nearly 2x prior year)
- **Top companies by valuation:** Anduril ($30.5B), Helsing ($12B), Chaos Industries ($4.5B), Saronic ($4B), Shield AI ($2.8B)

### Bessemer's Five Frontiers for 2026 (VC Perspective)
1. **Autonomy** - concept to combat, heterogeneous swarm C2
2. **AI permeation of DoD workflows** - mission-critical agentic AI
3. **Advanced manufacturing** - low-cost scalable munitions production
4. **Edge and network resilience** - contested environment comms
5. **Energy and materials independence** - critical minerals, energy security

## 3. Set-Aside Categories That Matter Most

### FY2024 Actual Performance (Government-Wide)

| Category | FY2024 Dollars | Goal | Achieved |
|----------|---------------|------|----------|
| **Total Small Business (prime)** | $183B (28.8% of all federal contracting) | 23% | Exceeded |
| **Small Disadvantaged Business (SDB)** | $76.2B (12.26%) | 12% (Biden-era elevated goal) | Exceeded |
| **Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned (SDVOSB)** | $31.9B | 3% | Far exceeded |
| **Women-Owned (WOSB)** | ~$1.1B in set-asides | 5% | Challenging |
| **HUBZone** | <$1B in set-asides | 3% | Consistently underperforms |

### FY2025 Statutory Goals (Reset to Minimums)

**Prime Contracting:**
- Small Business: 23.17%
- SDB: 5% (down from 12-13% elevated goal)
- WOSB: 5%
- SDVOSB: 5% (increased from 3%)
- HUBZone: 3%

**Subcontracting:**
- Small Business: 30%
- SDB: 5%
- WOSB: 5%
- SDVOSB: 5%
- HUBZone: 3%

### Which Set-Asides Matter Most for Defense?

**Tier 1 - Highest Value:**
1. **Small Business (general)** - $33.32B in set-aside contracts in FY2024. The bread and butter.
2. **8(a) Program** - $15.4B in sole-source and competed set-asides. Sole-source threshold increased to $30M (up from $25M as of Oct 2025). Most powerful tool for winning contracts without full competition.
3. **SDVOSB** - $8.5B in set-asides. Strong growth, goal increased from 3% to 5%. Defense agencies particularly strong here given veteran workforce.

**Tier 2 - Important but Smaller:**
4. **WOSB** - $1.1B in set-asides. Underperforming vs goals, meaning agencies are under pressure to increase awards.
5. **HUBZone** - <$1B in set-asides. Consistently underperforms, but agencies are graded on it. Geographic constraints limit applicability.

**Key Insight:** The 8(a) program remains the most powerful set-aside for small defense companies due to sole-source authority. SDVOSB is the fastest growing and most natural fit for defense. WOSB and HUBZone represent underserved areas where agencies need to increase awards (opportunity for qualifying firms).

### OASIS+ Set-Aside Awards (2024-2025)
- **8(a):** 127 businesses selected across 7 domains
- **SDVOSB:** 345 awardees
- **WOSB:** 309 awardees
- **HUBZone:** 113 awardees
- **Total Small Business:** 182 additional businesses in rolling awards
- **Domains:** Management & Advisory, Technical & Engineering, Intelligence, R&D, Logistics, Facilities, Environmental (expanding to 13 domains in Phase II)

## 4. Key Contract Vehicles for Small Defense Companies

### Entry-Level Vehicles

| Vehicle | Typical Size | Timeline | Best For |
|---------|-------------|----------|----------|
| **SBIR Phase I** | $50K-$250K | 3-6 months | Technology feasibility, getting a foot in the door |
| **SBIR Phase II** | $750K-$2M | 12-18 months | Prototype development |
| **SBIR Phase III** | Up to $30M (proposed) | Open-ended | Production transition, sole-source |
| **STTR Phase I** | $110K (AF) | 3-6 months | University-partnered R&D |
| **STTR Phase II** | Up to $1.8M (AF) | 12-18 months | University-partnered prototyping |
| **STRATFI** | $3M-$15M | Varies | Scaling SBIR tech with matching funds |
| **TACFI** | $375K-$1.7M | Varies | Bridging the valley of death |

**CRITICAL NOTE:** SBIR/STTR program authorization expired October 1, 2025. New activity is paused; ongoing awards remain valid. Congress is working on reauthorization. This is a major disruption to the small business innovation pipeline.

### Innovation / Rapid Acquisition Vehicles

| Vehicle | Scale | Key Feature |
|---------|-------|-------------|
| **OTA (Other Transaction Authority)** | $15.6B total DoD OTA spend in FY2023 | No FAR requirements, 92% have non-traditional performers |
| **DIU Prototype Contracts** | 450 awards since 2016, $1.7B total | 90-day prototype contracts, 51% transition to production |
| **AFWERX Open Topic** | ~$7.24B total since inception (10,400+ contracts) | Air Force innovation pathway |
| **SpaceWERX** | $460M annual budget | Space Force innovation |
| **CDAO Tradewinds** | $200M awarded in 2025 | AI/ML commercial solutions |
| **CSO (Commercial Solutions Opening)** | Varies | DIU's primary solicitation mechanism |

### Major IDIQ/GWAC Vehicles

| Vehicle | Ceiling | Set-Aside Pools | Domains |
|---------|---------|----------------|---------|
| **OASIS+** | $172.4B (unrestricted) | SB, 8(a), SDVOSB, WOSB, HUBZone | 13 domains (Phase II expansion) |
| **T4NG** | Multi-billion | Small business | IT services |
| **SEWP V** | $65B+ | Small business pools | IT hardware/services |

| **Army PIF IV** | $6B | Small business | Prototype integration |

### OTA Statistics
- **FY2023:** 2,532 OTAs awarded (up from 2,091 in FY2021)
- **Total DoD OTA spend FY2023:** $15.6B ($9.3B non-IT, $6.3B IT)
- **92%** of OTAs include non-traditional defense contractors
- **FY2025 NDAA** lowered approval threshold for transactions up to $500M

### DIU Statistics
- **450 prototype awards** to 389 unique vendors since 2016
- **$1.7B in prototype awards**, $5.5B in production awards
- **51% of completed prototypes** transitioned to production
- **FY2024 funding:** $983M (431% increase from prior year)
- **FY2026 budget request:** $979M

## 5. Dollar Ranges Where Small Companies Compete

### Contract Size Distribution

| Range | Description | Competition Level | Vehicle |
|-------|-------------|-------------------|---------|
| **$3K-$15K** | Micro-purchase (threshold raised to $15K in Oct 2025) | Low - no set-aside required | Government purchase card |
| **$15K-$350K** | Simplified acquisition (threshold raised to $350K in Oct 2025) | Moderate - **reserved for small business** if 2+ capable sources | SAP procedures |
| **$250K-$5M** | Typical small business competed contracts | High | Full & open, set-asides |
| **$50K-$250K** | SBIR Phase I | Moderate | SBIR |
| **$750K-$2M** | SBIR Phase II | Moderate | SBIR |
| **$1M-$25M** | Sweet spot for established small defense cos | High | IDIQ task orders, set-asides |
| **$3M-$15M** | STRATFI bridge funding | Moderate | AFWERX/SpaceWERX |
| **$25M-$100M** | Upper range for mature small businesses | Very high | 8(a) sole-source (up to $30M), IDIQ |
| **$100M+** | Rare for small businesses; usually teaming/JV | Extreme | Major IDIQs, mentor-protege |

### Key Statistics
- **Average small business contract in FY2024:** $2.7M (9% increase YoY)
- **Total small business prime contracts:** $183B to ~79,000 companies
- **Average per company:** ~$2.3M
- **Simplified acquisition threshold contracts:** ~230,000 awards in this range at any given time (FY2022-2024)
- **Micro-purchase awards:** ~560,000 annual awards to ~18,000 companies
- **8(a) sole-source ceiling increased** from $25M to $30M (Oct 2025) - significant for larger small businesses

### Where the Money Actually Is
- **$176.11B** went to small businesses from DoD and civilian agencies combined (23.3% of $773.68B total)
- **Defense agencies accounted for 59.87%** ($464.2B) of all federal contracts
- **GWACs/IDIQs:** $74.8B awarded through these vehicles (9.5% of total) - task orders are where ongoing work flows
- **Small business set-asides specifically:** $33.32B

## Summary: Where Small Defense Companies Should Focus

### Highest-Opportunity Quadrants

**High Budget + High Small Business Accessibility:**
1. IT Services & Cybersecurity (NAICS 541512, 541519, 541511)
2. Engineering/Technical Services (NAICS 541330)
3. Counter-UAS technologies ($3.1B and growing 43% YoY)
4. AI/ML applications for defense workflows
5. Construction & facilities (NAICS 236220)

**Growing Fast + Small Business Friendly:**
1. Autonomous systems (via SBIR/OTA/DIU)
2. Space technologies (via SpaceWERX)
3. CMMC compliance tools and services (338K contractors need help)
4. Edge computing / contested environment tech
5. Logistics and supply chain modernization

### Recommended Entry Strategy for an Underdog
1. **Get certified** - 8(a) or SDVOSB certification opens the most doors
2. **Start with SBIR** (when reauthorized) or OTA prototype contracts
3. **Target $500K-$5M range** initially - sweet spot for small business set-asides
4. **Focus on services** over products (8 of top 10 PSCs are services)
5. **Build toward OASIS+** qualification for long-term pipeline
6. **Pick a hot tech area** - counter-UAS, AI/ML, cybersecurity, autonomy
7. **Target Army and DISA** - largest volume of small business-friendly awards
