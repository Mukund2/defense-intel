# Mission

The defense industry is broken. The same incumbents — Boeing, Lockheed, Raytheon — have held the keys for decades. They overcharge, underdeliver, and when a faster, cheaper, better company threatens their position, they sue (see: Boeing v. Anduril). They don't compete on merit. They compete on inertia, lobbyists, and lock-in.

This matters because national security isn't a jobs program for defense primes. When an F-35 is $100M over budget and 7 years late, that's not just waste — that's capability our warfighters don't have. When a 20-person startup builds a better drone for 1/10th the cost but can't navigate the procurement maze, the wrong company wins the contract and the mission suffers.

**Our goal is simple: get contracts to the companies that deserve them.**

Small defense companies — the ones actually innovating — lose contracts they're qualified for because they don't have the BD infrastructure, the capture teams, or the institutional knowledge that primes have built over 50 years. We level that playing field.

Every feature we build, every decision we make, comes back to one question: **does this help an underdog win a contract they deserve?**

If it doesn't, we don't build it.
