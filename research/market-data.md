# Defense Small Business Contracting: Market Data & Analysis

> Research compiled March 2026. Sources include SBA, GAO, DoD OSBP, FPDS, USAspending, and SBIR.gov data.

---

## 1. Small Business Share of Defense Contracts — Trend Over Time

### Federal Government-Wide Small Business Prime Contract Awards

| Fiscal Year | $ Awarded to Small Biz | % of Eligible Dollars | Gov-Wide Goal |
|-------------|----------------------|----------------------|---------------|
| FY2020      | $145.7B              | ~26.5%               | 23%           |
| FY2021      | $154.2B              | ~27.2%               | 23%           |
| FY2022      | $162.9B              | ~26.5%               | 23%           |
| FY2023      | $178.6B              | 28.4%                | 23%           |
| FY2024      | $183.5B              | 28.8%                | 23%           |

**Key takeaway:** Four consecutive years of record-breaking growth. Dollar amounts up 26% from FY2020 to FY2024.

### DoD-Specific Performance

- DoD's small business goal: **22.43%** (FY2024), rising to **23.17%** (FY2025)
- DoD earned an **"A"** on the FY2024 SBA Small Business Scorecard
- DoD **increased small business awards by $4.9B** in FY2024 over the prior year
- Total DoD contract spending: **~$455B** in FY2024 (~61% of all federal contracts)
- Estimated DoD small business awards: **~$100-105B** (based on ~22-23% of $455B)

### The Paradox: Dollars Up, Companies Down

- In FY2022, **58,681 small businesses** received federal contracts -- **fewer than half** the number that received contracts in 2010
- The defense industrial base has been **shrinking every year since 2017**: from 76,700 companies to ~60,000 by 2021
- **80% drop** in new business entrants seeking to work with DoD over the past 15 years
- Prime contractors consolidated from **51 to fewer than 10** over the past 30 years

**Implication:** More money chasing fewer companies = massive opportunity for new entrants who can navigate the system.

## 2. Set-Aside Program Dollar Flows

### FY2024 Socioeconomic Category Breakdown

| Program | FY2024 $ Awarded | % of Eligible | Goal | Met? |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|------|------|
| Small Business (overall) | $183.5B | 28.8% | 23% | YES |
| Small Disadvantaged Business (SDB/8(a)) | $78.1B | ~12%+ | 5% (statutory baseline, was 13% under EO) | YES |
| Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned (SDVOSB) | $32.8B | 5.14% | 5% (raised from 3% in FY2024 NDAA) | YES |
| Women-Owned (WOSB) | $26.6B | 3.44% | 5% | NO |
| HUBZone | $17.6B | 2.75% | 3% | NO |

### Set-Aside Specifics

- **8(a)/SDB:** $78.1B is the broader SDB category. The 8(a) program is a subset. SDB goal was lowered back to statutory 5% baseline in Jan 2025 after EO 14091 was repealed (had been 13% for FY2024)
- **SDVOSB:** $32.8B awarded, up $900M from FY2023. New requirement: SBA certification required (self-certification ended Aug 2024). The goal increase from 3% to 5% creates a **$31B+ annual target**
- **WOSB:** $26.6B, up $1.15B from FY2023, but still short of 5% goal. WOSB set-aside programs accounted for **less than $1.3B** of total WOSB awards -- most awards come through open competition, not set-asides
- **HUBZone:** $17.6B awarded. Government has **never consistently met the 3% goal**, though awards have increased every year since 2018

### Sole-Source Awards
- Total sole-source to small businesses: **$30.3B** (3.9% of contract obligations)
- Sole-source thresholds: **$5M to $8.5M** depending on set-aside category

## 3. DoD Small Business Goaling: Target vs. Actual

### Government-Wide Scorecard (FY2024)

- **21 of 24 agencies** received an "A" or "A+" grade
- Only **NASA, HHS, and USAID** failed to meet their goals
- Grading: A+ = 120%+ of goal, A = 100-119%, B = 90-99%, C = 80-89%, D = 70-79%, F = <70%

### FY2024 Category Performance (Government-Wide)

| Category | Goal | Achieved | Delta |
|----------|------|----------|-------|
| Small Business | 23% | 28.8% | +5.8pp |
| SDB | 13% (EO) / 5% (statutory) | ~12%+ | Met statutory, near EO target |
| SDVOSB | 5% (new) | 5.14% | +0.14pp |
| WOSB | 5% | 3.44% | -1.56pp (MISSED) |
| HUBZone | 3% | 2.75% | -0.25pp (MISSED) |

**Key insight:** WOSB and HUBZone are **consistently underserved categories**. The government misses these goals nearly every year, which means:
1. There's political pressure to increase awards in these categories
2. Companies with these certifications face less competition for set-asides

## 5. Number of Small Defense Companies

### SAM.gov Registrations
- **650,000+** active entities registered in SAM.gov
- Not all are defense-focused; this includes all federal contractor registrations

### Small Businesses Winning Contracts (FY2024)
- **78,747 small businesses** won federal contracts in FY2024
- These represent **71.7%** of the 108,899 total entities that won contracts
- Of these, **fewer than 18%** won contracts worth more than $1M

### Defense Industrial Base Specifically
- ~**60,000 companies** in the defense industrial base (as of 2021, down from 76,700 in 2017)
- Net loss of **3,300 companies** in FY2021 alone, though 8,300+ new firms entered that year (high churn)
- **400,000+ small businesses** registered to perform federal contracts, but only ~58,000 actually received awards in FY2022

### Veteran-Owned
- Veteran-owned firms represent **~10%** of all registered federal contractors

## 6. Average Small Defense Company Win Rate

### Industry Benchmarks
- **Average proposal win rate: ~33%** (industry-wide, proposals submitted vs. won)
- This is the benchmark for companies that have an established capture process

### Reality for Most Small Businesses
- Of 400,000+ registered small businesses, only **~58,000-78,000** actually win contracts in any given year
- That's a **~15-20% win rate** just in terms of "registered companies that win anything"
- **Less than 18%** of small businesses winning contracts get awards over $1M
- Contracting officers often **favor larger vendors**, perceiving them as more reliable

### What Drives Higher Win Rates
- Companies with mature business development processes win at 40-60%+
- Set-aside designations dramatically improve odds (fewer competitors)
- Incumbent advantage is enormous -- re-compete win rates are much higher than new-bid win rates
- Relationship-building with contracting officers before RFPs drop is critical

## 7. Uncontested Contracts and Low-Bidder Situations

### FY2024 Competition Data (Federal-Wide)

**This is a staggering finding:**
- **81% of federal contracts** had zero competition (single offer received)
- **91% of the time**, contracts were awarded to 1 of 5 companies competing
- In 2019, "Not Reported" and "ONE Offer" categories totaled just 36% -- this has **more than doubled**

### Why This Happens
- FAR 6.3 allows contracting officers to limit competition via "Other than Full and Open Competition"
- Officers gravitate toward vendors they "know, like, and trust"
- Niche technical requirements naturally limit the bidder pool
- Incumbency advantage -- hard to unseat an existing contractor

### Low-Competition NAICS Codes (Near-Zero Bidder Rivalry)

| NAICS Code | Description | Avg Bids/Solicitation | Notes |
|------------|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| 623110 | Nursing Care Facilities | ~1.0 | ~$1B in awards |
| 333997 | Scale & Balance Manufacturing | ~1.0 | 318 awards |
| 334614 | Software & Pre-Recorded Media Mfg | ~1.0 | 58 awards |
| 453210 | Office Supplies & Stationery | ~1.01 | 100K+ micro-purchases |

### High-Competition NAICS Codes (For Contrast)
- Janitorial Services: **7.07 bids/solicitation** average
- Routine service contracts: often **10+ offers**

**Key insight:** The government is **actively seeking new bidders** in low-competition areas to reduce single-source dependency. Low competition = high leverage for new entrants.

## 8. Underserved Niches and Opportunity Areas

### Structural Gaps

1. **WOSB and HUBZone categories** -- government consistently misses goals, creating demand for certified firms
2. **Cybersecurity and IT modernization** -- DoD spending heavily but small business participation lags in advanced technical areas
3. **Niche manufacturing** -- 90% of tactical missiles from 3 sources, satellites similarly concentrated
4. **Supply chain resilience** -- DoD actively seeking to diversify suppliers after COVID and Ukraine exposed vulnerabilities

### Where Small Businesses Win Most (FY2024 Top Service Categories)

Small businesses dominate in:
- **Facilities & Construction** (17.07% of total contract awards)
- **Professional Services** (IT consulting, management consulting, engineering support)
- **IT Services** (help desk, system administration, software development)
- All **top 10 PSCs** for small business in FY2024 were **service categories** -- no product/manufacturing codes

### FY2024 Spending Split (DoD)
- **54% Services** / **46% Products**
- Small businesses are overrepresented in services, underrepresented in products

### Specific Opportunity Signals
- Government **eagerly seeks new bidders** in categories with single-source dependency
- **Emerging tech domains** (AI/ML, autonomous systems, quantum, space) are being pushed through OTAs and SBIR, which favor small/nontraditional companies
- **Software/digital services** -- DoD's push for software factories and DevSecOps creates openings

## 9. SBIR/STTR Program Data

### Overall Program Scale

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| FY2024 total SBIR spending | $4.3B |
| FY2023 total SBIR spending (peak) | $4.4B |
| Unique companies awarded (FY2024) | 3,771 |
| Phase I awards (recent year) | 3,223 awards / $568M |
| Phase II awards (recent year) | 1,871 awards / $2.0B |

### Success Rates

| Phase | Success Rate |
|-------|-------------|
| Phase I | ~17-19% |
| Phase II (from Phase I winners) | ~60% |

### Award Thresholds (as of October 2024)
- Phase I: up to **$314,363** (without SBA approval)
- Phase II: up to **$2,095,748** (without SBA approval)

### DoD SBIR by Service Component
- **Air Force SBIR spending has dramatically outpaced Army and Navy** since mid-2010s
- Army: ~$387.7M in SBIR awards in FY2024, typically funds 10-13% of proposals received (~2,000/year)
- Air Force: typically provides 1,000-1,500 Phase I awards annually
- Army also ran **6 xTech competitions** in FY2024, awarding 86 follow-on SBIR contracts totaling $73M

### Concentration Trends
- Top 25 SBIR award winners received **16% of DoD SBIR dollars in 2011**, declining to **11% in 2024**
- This deconcentration suggests the program is becoming **more accessible** to new entrants

### 11 Federal Agencies Participate
Each agency releases its own topics/subtopics with unique success rates.

## 10. OTA (Other Transaction Authority) Data

### Scale and Growth

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| FY2024 total OTA obligations | **$18.3B+** |
| FY2024 prototype OTA obligations | $16B+ |
| FY2024 production OTA obligations | $2B |
| FY2019-2021 consortia OTA obligations | $24B+ (nearly 2/3 of all prototype OTAs) |

### Small Business / Nontraditional Contractor Participation

- **93% of FY2024 OTA obligations** went to contracts citing "significant participation" by nontraditional defense contractors (NDCs)
- Only **7%** went to traditional contractors without NDC participation
- Selected consortia awarded OTAs to **NDC prime contractors in 50-60% of projects** (FY2021-2023)
- **60%+ of consortium awards in 2024** went to non-traditional companies (NSTXL data)

### Why OTAs Matter for Small Companies
- OTAs bypass FAR (Federal Acquisition Regulation) -- faster, more flexible
- Designed specifically to attract **nontraditional and small companies**
- No requirement for cost accounting standards or certified cost/pricing data
- Follow-on production is available: 284 of 1,776 prototype projects (16%) used OT for production

### Key Policy Development
- **FY2025 NDAA Section 888** directs DoD to establish a process by December 2025 to formally track NDC and small business awards through OTAs
- This signals **growing congressional interest** in ensuring OTAs deliver on their small business promise
- GAO noted that DoD's OTA tracking data is **inconsistent and incomplete**, creating both risk and opportunity

## Summary: Market Validation

### The Market is Real -- and Growing

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Total federal small business contract market | **$183.5B/year** (FY2024) |
| Estimated DoD small business market | **~$100-105B/year** |
| Year-over-year growth | **~5-8% annually** since FY2020 |
| OTA market (favoring small/nontraditional) | **$18.3B/year** and growing |
| SBIR/STTR funding | **$4.3B/year** |

### Why Now is Optimal Timing

1. **Shrinking vendor base + growing dollars** = less competition per dollar
2. **80% fewer new entrants** over 15 years = wide open for new players
3. **81% of contracts get single bids** = winning is possible without beating 10 competitors
4. **WOSB and HUBZone goals consistently missed** = agencies under pressure to award to certified firms
5. **OTAs explicitly designed for nontraditional companies** = $18B+ market with 60%+ going to non-traditional firms
6. **SBIR deconcentrating** = top winners getting smaller share, more room for newcomers
7. **DoD actively seeking supply chain diversification** = political and strategic tailwinds

### Highest-Opportunity Niches

1. **IT/Software/Cybersecurity services** -- highest DoD spend category, service-oriented, small-business-friendly
2. **SDVOSB-certified firms** -- $32.8B market, newly increased 5% goal, certification barrier protects from competition
3. **OTA consortia participation** -- fastest-growing contract vehicle, explicitly favors nontraditional
4. **SBIR Phase I in emerging tech** -- $314K awards with 17% success rate, pathway to Phase II ($2M+) and Phase III (unlimited)
5. **Low-competition NAICS codes** -- manufacturing and niche services with ~1 bidder per solicitation
6. **HUBZone-certified firms** -- $17.6B market, goal consistently missed, agencies under pressure
