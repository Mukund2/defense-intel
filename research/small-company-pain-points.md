# Small Defense Company Pain Points: Real Research Findings

*Research compiled: March 2026*
*Sources: GAO reports, NDIA Vital Signs surveys, NSBA comments, congressional testimony, industry publications, founder accounts, tool reviews, federal data*

---

## Executive Summary

The defense industrial base has lost 40% of its small businesses over the past decade. 66% of small business respondents identify the complexity of federal acquisition as their #1 challenge. Small businesses held 61% of contracts terminated by DOGE in 2025. The problems are structural, not cosmetic — and the existing tool landscape is either too expensive, too complex, or too narrow to address what small companies actually need.

---

## 1. THE REAL PAIN POINTS (Ranked by Severity)

### 1.1 The Opportunity Discovery & Intelligence Gap

**The Problem**: Small companies don't know what's coming, can't find what's relevant, and can't afford the tools that would help.

- SAM.gov is the free baseline but is universally described as hard to use, poorly searchable, and lacking context
- GovWin IQ (Deltek) is the industry standard but costs $13,000-$119,000/year (avg ~$29,000). User reviews call it "a financial strain on small businesses" with "exorbitant price"
- GovWin is essentially "a web scraper gathering opportunities from various online sources" with a UI that's "not the most user-friendly" and data that "often lacks verification or refinement"
- Information comes from multiple disconnected sources — SAM.gov, FPDS, GSA eBuy, agency-specific portals — and no single affordable tool aggregates them well
- Winners engage agencies 6-12 months before an RFP is released; small companies without intelligence tools are always playing catch-up

**What people actually say**: "The information on how to do this — I've never found one place where it all exists. You can go to SBA.gov and there's some great information there but it doesn't give you everything that you need, not even close."

**Willingness to pay**: NextStage (a newer entrant) charges $3,000/year and is getting traction with small businesses calling it "game changing." This suggests the price ceiling for small companies is roughly $3K-$10K/year, far below GovWin's pricing.

### 1.2 The Capture Management Process Gap

**The Problem**: Most small companies have no formal capture process, and those that do are using spreadsheets.

- Only 55% of companies have a formal capture management system in place
- Organizations with a dedicated capture process report win rates up to 50% higher
- Small BD teams use "Post-Its, emails, or spreadsheets because they are cheap" — but these systems "become outdated" and create information silos
- Handing over accurate, up-to-date data between BD and proposal teams is "often difficult"
- Each team maintains disconnected Excel trackers leading to "errors and data misinterpretation"

**The real gap**: Small companies (5-50 people) can't afford a TechnoMile ($$$, enterprise pricing, requires Salesforce) or Deltek CRM, but need something more structured than Excel. The tools in this space are either built for Fortune 500 defense primes or built for generic B2B sales (Salesforce, HubSpot) with no govcon-specific workflows.

**What would make someone switch**: A tool that understands the Shipley capture process, tracks gate reviews, manages color teams, and connects opportunity discovery to capture to proposal — without requiring a 6-month implementation or Salesforce subscription.

### 1.3 The Proposal Response Burden

**The Problem**: Writing proposals is the single most expensive and time-consuming activity for small defense companies, and the cost of losing is devastating.

- A "hefty RFP with a deadline requiring a response within four weeks" is common
- Proposal costs include: proposal managers, writers, SMEs, compliance reviewers, external consultants, travel for site visits/pre-bid meetings
- Full-scale proposal support is "particularly beneficial for bids valued over $10M with multiple volumes" — but small companies bidding $1-10M contracts face the same complexity with smaller margins
- Small business protests at GAO are "far more likely to be denied than those filed by larger companies"
- AI tools are beginning to "reduce the burden of manual proposal writing, cutting timelines by 30-50%"

**The real gap**: Proposal consultants charge $150-300/hr. A small company might spend $50K-$150K on a single proposal response for a $5M contract. If they lose, that's potentially fatal. AI-assisted proposal writing is emerging (ProposalHelper, GovDash) but no tool yet does the full lifecycle well — from compliance matrix parsing to volume writing to color team management.

### 1.4 Cash Flow & Payment Delays

**The Problem**: The government is slow to pay, and small companies don't have the cash reserves to absorb it.

- The Prompt Payment Act requires 30-day payment, but validation can take "over a month" before that clock starts
- As subcontractors, payment terms are even worse: primes often pay "when the prime is paid by the government, which can delay payment to the subcontractor for 60-90 days"
- Many small businesses "lack the upfront cash necessary to accept these projects" and face "added expenses for chasing invoices, inability to cover expenses like rent and payroll"
- Since 2008, regulations have "prevented small businesses' access to capital through the typical borrowing route"
- Many resort to receivables financing companies, "which is an expensive proposition"

**Tool opportunity**: This is more of a fintech problem than a SaaS problem, but cash flow forecasting tied to contract milestones and invoice tracking is a gap in existing govcon tools.

### 1.5 CMMC & Compliance Burden

**The Problem**: The cost of compliance is literally driving small companies out of defense.

- CMMC Level 2 certification costs $100,000-$200,000 for most small/midsized defense contractors
- DoD lost 43% of its small business contractors between 2016-2022
- Even CMMC 2.0 (simplified from 5 levels to 3) hasn't gone far enough per SBA
- The Small Business Cybersecurity Act of 2024 proposes up to $50,000 in tax credits for companies with <50 employees — acknowledging the cost is unsustainable
- Beyond CMMC: FAR compliance, DFARS, CAS, ITAR/EAR export controls, facility clearances — the regulatory stack is "comparable to the size of the tax code"

**The real gap**: No affordable tool helps small companies navigate the full compliance landscape. They need a "what do I need for THIS contract?" answer, not a generic compliance framework.

### 1.6 Security Clearance & SCIF Access

**The Problem**: You can't bid on classified work without a facility clearance, and you can't get a facility clearance without a classified contract.

- 44% of defense industry companies rank "accessing classified environments as the greatest barrier to working with the government"
- SCIF construction costs range from $200,000 to $1M+
- Personnel clearance sponsorship costs $12,000-$25,000 per person
- A small business "cannot seek out a facility security clearance without first having a cleared contract to work on" — classic chicken-and-egg
- "Classified Spaces as a Service" (CSaaS) is emerging (Westway, Nooks) but still limited geographically

**Tool opportunity**: Limited — this is an infrastructure/policy problem. But a tool that maps which opportunities require clearances and which don't, and connects companies to shared SCIF providers, would help with the discovery side.

### 1.7 The Past Performance Catch-22

**The Problem**: You need past performance to win contracts, but you need contracts to get past performance.

- This is universally cited as the #1 barrier for new entrants
- Solutions include: teaming/subcontracting (most common), SBIR/STTR programs ($50K-$1.5M), mentor-protege programs
- "Small businesses often break into new fields through teaming agreements — subcontracting and serving on the team of a prime contractor"
- It can take "up to 18 months to win your first DoD contract"

**Tool opportunity**: A tool that systematically identifies teaming opportunities — matching complementary capabilities, past performance gaps, and upcoming opportunities — would be high-value. SUBNet (SBA's subcontracting database) exists but is poorly maintained.

### 1.8 The Teaming & Partner Discovery Problem

**The Problem**: Finding the right teaming partners is critical but ad hoc and relationship-dependent.

- "If you don't invest time into selecting the right partners, you increase your chances of losing"
- "Many companies rely on informal networks rather than systematic evaluation"
- Navigating SBA affiliation rules for teaming is complex and risky
- Finding contractors who "have won related contracts but lack specific capabilities you possess identifies natural teaming partners" — but doing this research manually is extremely time-consuming

**Tool opportunity**: High. This is essentially a matching problem that data can solve. Tools like Teaming Pro exist but the market is early.

- **Email** for team coordination

- **Word/Google Docs** for proposal writing
- **Outlook calendar** for deadline tracking
- **Personal network/phone calls** for teaming
- **LinkedIn** for finding contacts
- **Literally nothing** structured for capture management

## 3. THE 2025-2026 CRISIS: DOGE & BUDGET CHAOS

This deserves its own section because it's fundamentally changed the landscape.

### What happened:
- DOGE terminated 5,232+ contract actions worth $482M+ in de-obligations at DoD alone
- **61% of terminated contracts were held by small businesses** — massively disproportionate impact
- Full multi-year value of terminated small business contracts potentially in the billions
- Secretary Hegseth ordered a review of ALL small business set-aside contracts over $20M at DoD (Jan 2026)
- DoD's 8(a) spending is "nearly 10 times that of any other agency" — making it the biggest target

### What this means for small companies:
- Existing contracts are no longer safe — they can be terminated for convenience at any time
- New solicitations are delayed "well into the Spring" due to three concurrent budget cycles
- The continuing resolution (10 of the last 11 fiscal years) prevents new program starts and production increases
- Small businesses are diversifying away from pure defense dependence
- **Fear and uncertainty is the dominant emotion** — "the early signals point to a shifting procurement landscape that demands agility, diversification, and vigilance"

### Tool implications:
- Contract monitoring/early warning is now a critical need (is my contract at risk?)
- Diversification intelligence (what adjacent federal or commercial markets can I pursue?)
- Budget tracking tied to specific programs and agencies
- DOGE termination tracking and analysis

## 4. THE VALLEY OF DEATH (SBIR-Specific)

**The single most complained-about structural problem in defense innovation:**

- Only ~5% of companies make it from SBIR Phase II to Phase III
- Only 16% of companies that got an early-stage investment contract made it to Phase III procurement
- The gap between Phase II ($750K-$1.5M prototype) and production contracts ($10M+) has no consistent funding bridge
- STRATFI ($3-15M) and TACFI ($375K-$1.7M) exist to bridge this gap but are underutilized and hard to access
- APFIT provides procurement funding for capabilities ready to transition — but awareness is low

**Tool opportunity**: SBIR/STTR tracking, Phase III transition planning, and matchmaking with Programs of Record that need the technology. Currently this is done through personal relationships and luck.

## 5. WHAT WOULD MAKE SOMEONE SWITCH FROM THEIR CURRENT PROCESS?

Based on patterns across all research:

### Must-haves (table stakes):
1. **Affordable** — under $5K/year for a 5-person BD team, under $10K for 10-20 people
2. **Fast to implement** — days, not months. NextStage's "1-2 weeks" sets the benchmark
3. **No Salesforce dependency** — small companies don't have Salesforce
4. **Aggregated opportunity search** — SAM.gov + FPDS + agency portals in one place
5. **Pipeline tracking** that replaces Excel — with govcon-specific stages (not generic B2B)

### Differentiators (what would actually get someone to pay):
1. **Competitive intelligence automation** — who won similar contracts, who's likely bidding, what's the incumbent's performance history
2. **Teaming partner matching** — based on complementary capabilities, past performance, and opportunity requirements
3. **Recompete and pre-RFP alerts** — knowing about opportunities 6-12 months before they hit SAM.gov
4. **Compliance navigator** — "what certifications/clearances do I need for THIS specific opportunity?"
5. **AI-assisted proposal outlines** — not full proposal writing, but compliance matrix parsing, outline generation, and win theme suggestions
6. **Contract risk monitoring** — is my agency/program at risk from DOGE, CR, or budget cuts?

### What nobody has nailed yet:
1. **The full BD lifecycle in one tool** — from opportunity discovery through capture through proposal through award tracking — at a small business price point
2. **Relationship intelligence** — who at the agency should I be talking to, who are the decision makers, what events are they attending
3. **Win/loss analysis** — systematic tracking of why you won or lost, with pattern recognition across bids
4. **Subcontracting opportunity discovery** — a better SUBNet that actually works and is kept current
5. **Program budget tracking** — connecting specific programs to budget line items so you know if funding is real

## 8. BOTTOM LINE: WHERE THE REAL GAPS ARE

The market has decent (if expensive) solutions for **opportunity discovery** and improving (but early) solutions for **AI-assisted proposals**.

The massive unsolved gaps are:

1. **Affordable, integrated BD lifecycle tool** — discovery + capture + proposals + tracking at <$5K/year. NextStage is closest but still young.

2. **Competitive & relationship intelligence** — who's bidding, who's the incumbent, who are the decision-makers, what's the agency's buying pattern. Currently this is tribal knowledge held by expensive consultants and former government employees.

3. **Teaming partner discovery & matching** — the #1 way small companies break in is through teaming, but finding partners is still done through personal networks and conferences.

4. **Contract risk & budget intelligence** — in the DOGE era, knowing if your contract/program is at risk is existential. No tool does this well.

5. **Compliance navigation** — "what do I need for THIS opportunity?" rather than generic CMMC/FAR education.

6. **The SBIR Valley of Death bridge** — connecting Phase II companies to Programs of Record, transition funding (STRATFI/TACFI/APFIT), and production pathways.

The company that solves #1 + #2 + #3 at a small-business price point would own this market. The incumbents (Deltek, TechnoMile) are too expensive and too complex. The new entrants (NextStage, GovDash, CLEATUS) are each attacking pieces but nobody has the full picture yet.
