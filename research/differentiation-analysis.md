# Differentiation Analysis: Us vs. Usul vs. The Field
*Research date: March 11, 2026*

---

## What Usul Actually Does (Stress-Tested)

Usul is the closest competitor. Here's what they ACTUALLY do vs. what they claim:

**Real product (confirmed):**
- Scrapes DoD data: contracts, budgets, PEO offices
- AI-powered contract inbox: daily recommendations matched to your company profile
- PEO directory: org charts, program managers, contracting officers with contact info
- Budget tracking: which programs have money for your type of product
- Competitive intelligence: competitor award history, component-level pricing
- Recompete alerts: notifications when contracts are approaching renewal
- Pipeline/CRM: track pursuits and stakeholder touchpoints

**Customers:** Firestorm, OneBrief, Overland AI — all growth-stage defense tech companies, not tiny startups
**Impact claimed:** $100M+ in contracts won by clients
**Funding:** $3.3M seed (Scout Ventures, YC S24)
**Team:** 11 people, ex-Palantir/Stanford founders
**Vision:** "Build the next 1000 defense primes" — expanding to NATO/AUKUS

**What Usul does NOT do (confirmed gaps):**
1. No proposal writing or RFP analysis
2. No compliance matrix generation
3. No win probability scoring or bid/no-bid recommendations
4. No pricing intelligence (what YOU should bid, not what competitors bid)
5. No capture strategy generation (win themes, differentiators, positioning)
6. No SBIR/STTR-specific guidance or valley-of-death navigation
7. No set-aside optimization (which certifications to leverage for which opportunities)

**Key observation:** Nobody in this space does all three of:
1. Defense-specific depth
2. Prescriptive capture strategy (not just data)
3. Focused on small/underdog companies specifically

Usul does #1 but is data/research-oriented, not prescriptive. GovDash does proposals but isn't defense-specific. Nobody does #3 explicitly.

## Real Pain Points (From Actual Sources)

### What small defense companies actually struggle with:

**1. 66% say acquisition process complexity is their #1 barrier** (National Defense Magazine survey)
- FAR spans thousands of pages
- Compliance requirements (CMMC, ITAR, FedRAMP) overwhelm small teams
- "The drawn-out procurement process can leave contracts outdated before they are finalized"

**2. They bid on too many things and win too few**
- 400,000+ small businesses registered in SAM.gov
- Only 78,677 received ANY contract in FY24
- 60.6% of winners got less than $100K
- Less than 18% won contracts worth more than $1M
- Companies spread thin across 20 RFPs instead of going deep on 3

**3. The Valley of Death is real — only 5% of SBIR Phase II companies reach Phase III**
- SBIR is the #1 entry point for defense tech startups
- $4.3B+ in annual SBIR/STTR funding
- But transitioning from SBIR grant to production contract is where 95% die
- Need to understand STRATFI, TACFI, CATALYST programs to bridge the gap
- Need PEO sponsorship and Program of Record alignment

**4. They don't engage pre-RFP**
- "80% of wins are determined before the RFP drops"
- Primes engage contracting officers during RFI/Sources Sought phase
- Small companies find out about opportunities when the RFP publishes — too late
- Industry days, capability briefings, white papers — small companies skip these

**5. Resource constraints**
- "Small firms lack dedicated proposal teams, often relying on a handful of staff or CEOs to manage the entire bidding process"
- Can't afford capture managers ($150K-250K/year)
- Can't afford Govini ($100K+ enterprise contracts) or GovWin

## Market Size (Hard Numbers)

- **$773.68B** in total federal contract awards FY24

- **78,677** small businesses received contracts in FY24
- **400,000+** small businesses registered in SAM.gov (so ~80% get nothing)
- **DoD goal:** 23% to small business (they're close but creative accounting)
- **Set-aside programs:** 8(a), HUBZone (~3,000 certified), SDVOSB, WOSB

- **OTAs:** 80%+ of DIU awards go to small businesses

## Stress-Testing Potential Differentiators

### Gap 1: "Prescriptive capture strategy, not just data"
**Claim:** Usul shows you data. We tell you what to do with it.
**Stress test:** Is this real or just marketing?

**Verdict: REAL but THIN.** Both use AI. The difference is:
- Usul: "Here are contracts matching your profile, ranked by fit score"
- Us: "Bid on this one. Skip that one. Here's why, here's the incumbent's weakness, here's how to price, here's your win theme."

The prescriptive layer is valuable but could be replicated by Usul adding a Claude wrapper on top of their data. **This alone is not a durable differentiator.**

### Gap 2: "Defense-only while they go wide"
**Claim:** Usul says defense-focused but monitors $8T across 60+ nations and multiple sectors.
**Stress test:** Are they actually going wide?

**Verdict: PARTIALLY REAL.** Usul is expanding to 60+ nations and multiple sectors (defense, IT, energy, healthcare). They're going broad. But their core product and customers (Firestorm, OneBrief, Overland AI) are still defense. They could snap back to defense-only if threatened. **Moderate differentiator, not durable.**

### Gap 3: "Proposal support + RFP analysis"
**Claim:** Usul doesn't do proposals. We do.
**Stress test:** Is this a real gap?

**Verdict: REAL.** Usul has zero proposal features. GovDash does proposals but isn't defense-specific. There's no product that does defense-specific proposal strategy informed by defense-specific capture intelligence. **Meaningful gap but GovDash could add defense depth.**

### Gap 4: "We serve underdogs specifically, not growth-stage companies"
**Claim:** Usul's customers are Firestorm, OneBrief, Overland AI — companies with VC funding and BD teams. We serve the 20-person company with one BD person.
**Stress test:** Is the underdog market real and big enough?

**Verdict: VERY REAL.**
- 400,000 small businesses registered in SAM.gov
- Only 78,677 got any contract in FY24
- 60% of winners got less than $100K
- These companies can't afford Usul's enterprise pricing, GovDash's custom quotes, or Govini's $100K contracts
- $149/mo self-serve is a completely different market segment
- **But:** These companies have small wallets. Can you build a business on $149/mo from companies that might not be around next year?

### Gap 5: "Valley of Death navigation — SBIR → Production"
**Claim:** Nobody helps companies bridge SBIR Phase II to Phase III/production.
**Stress test:** Is this a real product or just advice?

**Verdict: THIS IS THE MOST INTERESTING GAP.**
- 95% failure rate at Phase II → Phase III transition
- $4.3B+ in annual SBIR/STTR funding, overwhelmingly going to defense
- The transition requires: PEO sponsorship, Program of Record alignment, STRATFI/TACFI applications, understanding which programs have unfunded requirements your tech could fill
- **No tool automates this.** Companies hire consultants ($50K-200K) or fumble through it alone
- The data to support this exists: budget documents, PEO org charts, Program of Record data, STRATFI/TACFI announcements
- Usul has some of this data (PEO directory, budget tracking) but doesn't frame it as "here's how to cross the valley of death"

### Gap 6: "Set-aside optimization"
**Claim:** Help companies maximize their set-aside certifications.
**Stress test:** Is this real?

**Verdict: REAL BUT NARROW.**
- Many companies have set-aside eligibility they don't fully exploit
- 8(a) sole-source threshold is $4.5M for goods, $7M for services — many companies don't know this
- HUBZone has a 3% government-wide goal with only ~3,000 certified companies — low competition
- But this is more of a feature than a product. Useful within a broader platform.

## Synthesis: Where's the REAL Differentiation?

After stress-testing, here are the gaps ranked by durability and meaningfulness:

### Tier 1: Strong, Durable Differentiators

**1. SBIR Valley of Death Navigation (Gap 5)**
- Nobody does this as a product
- Huge market: thousands of SBIR Phase II companies stuck
- Real pain: 95% failure rate, companies hire $50K-200K consultants
- Data exists to automate it: PEO budgets, Program of Record data, STRATFI/TACFI
- Hard for Usul to pivot here — their customers (Firestorm, OneBrief) are past this stage
- **Moat:** Deep understanding of the SBIR→production pipeline, not just contract matching

**2. Self-Serve for True Underdogs (Gap 4)**
- Completely different market segment than Usul (growth-stage) or GovDash (enterprise)
- 400,000 registered small businesses, most getting nothing
- $149/mo vs. enterprise sales
- Risk: low ARPU, but massive volume potential

### Tier 2: Meaningful but Replicable

**3. Defense-Specific Proposal Strategy (Gap 3)**
- Real gap: no product does defense-specific proposal strategy informed by capture intel
- Replicable by GovDash (add defense depth) or Usul (add proposal features)
- Still worth building — first-mover advantage matters

**4. Prescriptive vs. Descriptive (Gap 1)**
- Real value: "do this" vs. "here's data"
- But thin as a standalone differentiator — Usul adds a Claude layer and they match us

### Tier 3: Features, Not Differentiators

**5. Set-aside optimization (Gap 6)** — Feature within platform
**6. Defense-only depth (Gap 2)** — Usul is already defense-focused
